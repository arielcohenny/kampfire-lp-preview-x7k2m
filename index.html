The problem

The follow-up is where the investment leaks out.

Premium events spend millions to get the right people into one room. Then they hand the week after to the same playbook every event uses — generic email, gallery link, hope. The room delivered. The week after didn't.

Generic content. Unopened email.

Recaps, thank-yous, vendor lists, agendas—there's nothing in a generic post-event email that gives guests a reason to engage. So it goes unopened. And when it is opened, it's ignored.

The gallery link that dies on delivery

A single bulk gallery link, sent to every attendee. They have to find themselves in thousands of photos — most don't bother. Even if they do, you have no idea who engaged, no way to bring them back.

A post-event report that says "we posted the photos"

When your CMO asks what the event drove, anecdotes aren't the answer. You need attendee-level data on who did what. Names, not narratives. Without it, the next sponsor pitch and budget defense both rest on goodwill.

If the standard playbook delivers neither engagement nor proof — what does?

The unlock

Photos are the hook. Not the hero.

The fix isn't a better email. It's a better hook. Every guest has the same I want to see what I looked like moment — they always click for their personal photos, delivered directly. No gallery to scroll, no faces to search. That click opens a measured engagement channel: sponsors, CTAs, surveys, next-year registration.

  • ✓

    Personal photos, delivered directly

    One selfie. Face-matched against every shot from the event. Each guest gets their own album — no scrolling, no searching.

  • ✓

    Captions that write themselves — in your voice

    Auto-generated from the photo, your narrative, and the brand or sponsor you want amplified. Every share goes out on-message.

  • ✓

    Per-attendee data, by default

    Who clicked what, when, and from where. Names, not aggregates — ready for the next sponsor pitch, sales call, or budget review.
